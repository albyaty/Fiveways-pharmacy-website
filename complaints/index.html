Complaints and feedback

Complaints and feedback

If you are unhappy with a service, communication, or aspect of care from Five Ways Pharmacy, please contact the pharmacy so the concern can be reviewed fairly and as promptly as possible.

Direct pharmacy contact Fair review Professional standards Privacy concerns supported
Call the pharmacy Email the pharmacy

Urgent health concerns should be handled through NHS 111, your GP, or 999 in an emergency rather than through the complaints route.

Start with the pharmacy

The quickest route is to contact Five Ways Pharmacy directly

Many concerns can be clarified or resolved quickly when the pharmacy team can review what happened and explain the next step.

  • Phone: 0121 630 2680
  • Email: pharmacy.frk99@nhs.net
  • Address: 192A Saint Vincent Street West, Ladywood, Birmingham, West Midlands, B16 8RP

Useful references

Other organisations may help if your concern remains unresolved

Five Ways Pharmacy should normally be contacted first. If you still need independent support afterwards, the relevant regulator or oversight body may be appropriate depending on the issue.

  • General Pharmaceutical Council (GPhC) for pharmacy professional standards concerns
  • Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) for data protection concerns
  • For NHS service complaints, the pharmacy team can explain the next local NHS complaints route where relevant
What to include

Share enough detail for the concern to be reviewed

  • Your name and the best way to contact you
  • The date, service, order, prescription, or conversation involved
  • What happened and why you are concerned
  • Any outcome or next step you are asking the pharmacy to consider
Who can raise a concern

Patients, carers, and authorised representatives

  • You can raise a concern about your own care or service experience
  • A parent, carer, or representative can raise a concern where appropriate
  • The pharmacy may need enough information to confirm authority before discussing confidential details
What happens next

Reviewed with care, fairness, and appropriate confidentiality

Five Ways Pharmacy aims to acknowledge concerns promptly and review them carefully. Some issues can be answered quickly, while others may need time to investigate fully.

Initial review

First step

The pharmacy team will review the concern, gather the relevant facts, and identify whether any immediate action is needed.

Follow-up

Keeping you informed

If more time is needed, the pharmacy should explain that and update you when a fuller response is available.

Privacy concerns

Information handling

If your concern is about personal information, the pharmacy can review it alongside the privacy policy and explain the next step.

If you remain unhappy

Independent routes

The appropriate regulator or oversight body may depend on whether the concern is about pharmacy standards, privacy, or an NHS service.

Questions

Complaints FAQs

Can I complain by email or phone?

Yes. You can contact Five Ways Pharmacy by email at pharmacy.frk99@nhs.net or by phone on 0121 630 2680.

Can someone complain on behalf of a patient?

Yes, where appropriate. The pharmacy may need enough information to confirm that it is suitable to discuss the matter with that person.

Where do I raise a pharmacy professional standards concern?

Concerns about pharmacy professional standards can also be raised with the General Pharmaceutical Council.

What if my concern is about personal data?

You can contact the pharmacy first. If you remain unhappy about data handling, you can also contact the Information Commissioner’s Office.
