2. What information we collect

Personal details: name, date of birth, address and postcode, phone number, email address, and — where relevant to a service — NHS number and GP practice details.

Patient details: where you order or pay on behalf of someone else, the patient’s name and relevant details.

Health information: information you give us when using a pharmacy service or relating to a prescription — for example medical conditions, medicines, allergies, pregnancy status, and any details needed to supply a medicine safely. This is “special category” data and is given extra protection in law.

Payment information: your payment is processed by Stripe. We do not see or store your full card number; we receive a confirmation, the amount, and limited details such as the cardholder name and the last four digits.

Booking information: the appointment details you provide when booking through our scheduling provider.

Account information: if you create an account, your email and a securely hashed password (managed by our authentication provider), plus any profile details you choose to save.

Website information: contact-form submissions, and basic technical data such as your IP address and limited cookie data (see section 9).

3. How we use your information

  • To provide pharmacy services, dispense and deliver prescriptions, and manage appointments;
  • To take payment and send you a receipt;
  • To contact you about your order, booking or enquiry;
  • To liaise with your GP or other healthcare professionals where needed for safe care;
  • To meet our professional, NHS and legal record-keeping obligations;
  • To keep the website and our services secure and prevent fraud.

4. Legal basis for processing

Under UK GDPR, our lawful bases for processing your information include:

  • Contract — to provide the service or supply you have requested and take payment;
  • Legal obligation — to meet our duties as a registered pharmacy (including medicines and record-keeping law);
  • Legitimate interests — to run the pharmacy, respond to enquiries, and keep our services secure;
  • Consent — where we ask for it, for example for certain communications;
  • Vital interests — in an emergency affecting someone’s health or safety.

For health (special category) data, we additionally rely on Article 9(2)(h) of the UK GDPR — the provision of health care and treatment by, or under the responsibility of, a health professional bound by a duty of confidentiality.

5. Who we share information with

We never sell your data or use it for third-party marketing. We share it only where necessary, with:

  • The NHS, your GP and other healthcare professionals — where needed for your care or required by NHS guidance;
  • Stripe — to process payments securely;
  • Our scheduling provider (Cal.com) — to manage appointment bookings;
  • Our hosting and infrastructure providers (Vercel; Supabase for accounts; Resend for transactional email) — to run the website, store account data and send emails;
  • Delivery couriers — where needed to deliver your medication;
  • Regulators, safeguarding or law-enforcement bodies — where we are legally required to do so.

Some providers may process data outside the UK. Where they do, we rely on appropriate safeguards (such as UK adequacy regulations or standard contractual clauses).

6. How long we keep information

We keep personal and health information only as long as necessary for the purposes above and to meet our legal and professional obligations. Pharmacy and clinical records are kept in line with the NHS Records Management Code of Practice (typically eight years from your last contact for adult records, or longer in specific circumstances). Records relating to the supply of medicines are kept for the periods required by law. Website enquiries are kept for a shorter period ([e.g. up to 12 months]). When information is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymise it.

7. How we protect your information

We use appropriate technical and organisational measures to keep your information safe, including access-controlled systems, encryption in transit, staff confidentiality training, secure storage, and limiting access to those who need it to provide your care.

8. Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you;
  • ask us to correct inaccurate data;
  • ask us to erase data (where this does not conflict with our legal or clinical duties);
  • restrict or object to certain processing;
  • request a copy of certain data in a portable format;
  • withdraw consent where we rely on it.

To exercise any of these, contact us using the details below. We will respond within one month.

9. Cookies & analytics
